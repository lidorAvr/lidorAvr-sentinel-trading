**Adjudication summary.** The dominant — effectively the **only** — manual-exclusion reason is **R1/R2**: `compute_closed_campaigns` derives the entire stat-countability of a manual campaign from a **single field (`initial_stop`) on a single row (`first_day.iloc[0]`)**, never consulting `stop_loss` and never the rest of the campaign. Whether the ≈12 are **LEGITIMATE** (genuinely no risk basis ever recorded — DATA_INCOMPLETE is then the *honest, correct* behaviour, DATA_CONTRACTS.md:7-9,117) or a **BUG** (a valid stop sits in `stop_loss` / a later row that the classifier structurally cannot see — a real manual edge erased) **turns entirely on a single structural fact we cannot read without a per-campaign field census**: *for the ≈12 excluded manual campaigns, is `stop_loss` (or a non-day-0 `initial_stop`) populated with a valid below-entry stop?*

- **If YES for any of the 12 → it is a confirmed money-relevant BUG** (R1): WR / Expectancy / Heat / the 4-gate are computed off an artificially-shrunk, possibly-biased 9-campaign sample; the founder's real manual edge is under-counted exactly as he suspects.
- **If NO for all 12 → it is LEGITIMATE** DATA_INCOMPLETE (old manual entries booked without any stop) — the 9 is honest, and the fix is data-completion + an honest "12 manual excluded — stop not recorded" disclosure, not a math change.

Given (a) the founder is **near-certain** he has far more than 9 legitimate manual closed trades, (b) `stop_loss` is a documented, populated trade-row field the classifier provably ignores (`:203`), and (c) the earlier-era manual convention plausibly used `stop_loss`, the **BUG hypothesis (R1) is live and must be treated as the priority** — but it is **provable only by the per-campaign field census**, which is a pure read (Tier-A C-1) and must run before any code is touched. We do **not** assert "confirmed bug" without that census; we assert: *the classifier has a real, narrow blind-spot (`stop_loss` unread, single-row read) that would cause exactly this 21→9 collapse if the founder's older stops live in `stop_loss`, and that is the single highest-probability explanation of his missing edge.*
